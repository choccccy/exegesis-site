# Exegesis Editing Guidelines

## 1. Core Principles

- **Lean prose:** Remove any word, phrase, or sentence that doesn't add necessary meaning, atmosphere, or rhythm.
- **Voice preservation:** Retain the author's cadence, syntax, and poetic strain, especially the balance of scientific precision and existential tone.
- **Character voice distinction:** Each character maintains unique linguistic patterns—Nchọ's casual lowercase and pragmatism, Agnès's formal French-inflected speech with occasional italicized terms, Obi-me's technical status updates and data formatting, V3N's radio protocol brevity, Antikythera's omnipresent formal announcements, etc. etc. etc.
- **Tense consistency:** Use present tense for ongoing scientific/cosmic commentary; use past tense only for completed actions or mythic history.
- **Human vs. Cosmic:** Highlight tension between cosmic phenomena and personal experience; emphasize awe and cost.
- **In-world authenticity:** Use a blend of real science terminology and plausible invented jargon, generally leaning towards the former.

---

## 2. Dialogue and Character Voice

- **Formatting consistency:** Use dialogue component tags (`<DB char="character">`) for all spoken lines to maintain character voice clarity and have them be properly formatted when they're published to the Exegesis website.
- **Character-specific patterns:**
  - **Antikythera:** Formal, omnipresent, technical language with cosmic scope
  - **Nchọ:** Lowercase starts, contractions, practical observations ("alr," "y'all," casual competence)
  - **Obi-me:** Technical readouts in code formatting (`> bay lock 1: ENGAGED`), emoji for simple responses (`> 🔒`), status updates over conversation
  - **V3N:** Radio protocol brevity, call signs, professional pilot terminology
  - **Agnès:** Formal structure with occasional French terms in italics (*merci*, *hélas*, *à bientôt*), scientific precision
- **Avoid over-explaining:** Let character relationships and technical capabilities emerge through action and brief exchanges, not exposition.
- **Multi-character balance:** In scenes with multiple characters, balance dialogue and action to keep all characters active and distinct without overcrowding.

---

## 3. Paragraph-Level Structure

- **Focus:** Each paragraph delivers a new image, insight, or tension; avoid redundancy.
- **Density:** Pack information; prefer implication to exposition.
- **Rhythm shifts:** Vary sentence lengths; use short sentences for revelation, awe, or dread.
- **Emotional pivot:** Alternate technical observations and subjective character experience.
- **Action-dialogue balance:** Interweave physical actions with dialogue to ground characters in space and maintain pacing.
- **Emotional resonance in technical contexts:** Find moments of human connection within procedural or technical scenes.

---

## 4. Sentence-Level Mechanics

- **No filler:** Cut adverbs, redundant adjectives, and exposition that repeats.
- **Concrete language:** Use plausible sci-fi terms and metrics; anchor the abstract.
- **Evocative details:** Choose vivid, cosmic-scale words.
- **Contrast:** Juxtapose scale, existential questions, and technical jargon.
- **Natural breaks:** Prefer splitting dense sentences for clarity.
- **Vary technical terminology:** Rotate terms (for example, when referring to propulsion, "thrusters," "RCS units," "maneuvering nozzles," "drive cone") and avoid repetitive phrasing within scenes.

---

## 5. Worldbuilding and Internal Consistency

- **Scientific analogues:** Ground invented terms in real physics convention when possible.
- **Proper nouns/jargon:** Make consistent, layered terms for new phenomena; use technical and colloquial forms.
- **Show, don't tell:** Favor scenes or reactions; let emotion emerge from fact or action.
- **Transhumanist subtlety:** Integrate posthuman elements naturally—distributed consciousness, body modularity, human-AI interface—without explicit exposition. Let technology feel lived-in, not marveled at. Keep in mind that all characters in the setting lack biological bodies, be they (post-)human, or AI.
- **Technical authenticity:** Use real aerospace, maritime, communication (proper radio protocols) and engineering, etc. terminology where applicable.
- **Technical procedure integration:** Weave operational sequences (startup, docking, system checks) into character interaction and cosmic atmosphere.
- **Cosmic scale grounding:** Anchor vast phenomena in immediate, physical experience.

---

## 6. Editing Workflow

1. **First pass:** Read for rhythm and emotional effect.
2. **Machete pass:** Cut extraneous words and details.
3. **Character voice pass:** Ensure each character's dialogue maintains their established patterns and linguistic quirks.
4. **Structure pass:** Reorder/combine sentences; break up dense ones.
5. **Voice check:** Ensure technical-poetic language; avoid blandness.
6. **Technical polish:** Review scientific terms and invented jargon for plausibility.
7. **Repetition check:** Scan for overused terms/repetitive sentences/etc., especially technical vocabulary, and vary appropriately.
8. **Final proof:** Correct spelling, grammar, and tense; check acronym usage and dialogue component formatting.

Throughout, include suggested rewrites in their local context as codeblocks, or the whole document if you're suggesting changes across the whole document, at the end of your messages.

---

## 7. Specifics from Past Suggestions

- **Scientific present for facts; past for myth/history/actions.**
- **Stay ruthless:** "Take a machete to verbosity."
- **Retain evocative adjectives:** "silent," "patient," "vast," etc.
- **Alternate cosmic horror and personal dread.**
- **Invent or adapt technical acronyms.**
- **Mix crew language and formal scientific description.**
- **Embed ship details in physical context.**
- **Maintain transhumanist atmosphere through casual references to distributed consciousness, body modularity, and seamless human-machine interface.**

---

## 8. Formatting

- **Dialogue components:** Use `<DB char="character">` tags consistently for all spoken dialogue.
- **Technical readouts:** Format AI/system outputs in code blocks within dialogue components.
- **Italics for foreign terms:** Use sparingly for established character voice (Agnès's French).
- **Paragraphs:** Maintain clear topic/emotional separation.
- **Image placeholders:** Include contextual image descriptions that enhance rather than repeat narrative content.
- **Technical lists:** Keep flat, never nested.
- Reference as 'Exegesis Editing Guidelines' for other tools.

---

**Summary:**  
Edit for density, clarity, technical accuracy, and existential/cosmic awe. Balance science and human scale. Trust the reader. Eliminate verbosity. Maintain distinct character voices through consistent linguistic patterns while integrating transhumanist themes subtly into the fabric of everyday operations.

Delta site was set up around a 13 kilometer wide hole that was immidiately identified near the equator of the Slumbering Empyrean, before Nysa's Wake even underwent its final insertion burn in the EK Draconis system; much larger holes were identified, but few matched its apparently bottomless depth.

This aspect was made even stranger by initial probe exploration, before crewed expeditions were greenlit by the Antikythera, which found a gravitational anomaly within the abyss. for the first 100 kilometers, there was minimal effect, but as probes travelled deeper, they were eventually completely unable to escape what appeared to be an event horizon in the narrowing chasm, at an estimated 1,400 kilometers below the surface of the Empyrean. Even if they weren't destroyed by the crushing gravity, primion exitation increased with depth into the hole, preventing exploration by even the simplest of drones by a depth of 350 kilometers.

Probes that weren't lost to this anomaly found that the walls of the hole appeared to be a cross section of the Slumbering Empyrean, showcasing hundreds of distinct structures, and thousands of openings, spanning from one side of the chasm to the other. Probe exploration of most of these tunnels found dead ends, while others seemed to serve some sort of circulatory function; most spouted some water into the void, but others occasionally dislodged other materials. 

One particularly notable pipe would occasionally spit a variable number of cobalt spheres into the chasm which contained between them an atmosphere of krypton at several GPa, while another would eject a glowing, white hot ferrous fluid at 2,482 °C that was magnetically contained as it floated across the hole into a receiving opening. Yet another, particularly large opening nearly 700 meters wide featured a cloud of dodecahedral diamonds with faces roughly 47 centimeters to a side, travelling across the chasm at several dozen kilometers per second into a receiving hole. Another hole just a few centimers wide appeared to disgorge a beam of protons at 99.999,999,999,4% of the speed of light, which actually intersects the diamond cloud; the diamonds which are struck by this beam are mostly annihilated. Hundreds of tubes seem to emit trace gasses and signifigant primion radiation.

Openings on the opposite side of the hole as an outputting hole appear to be safer, although many were occasionally found to "back up" and have their own, less common ejections. Yet more tunnels have no evident function, and are the primary candidates for exploration.

5 teams ventured into the depths at Delta Site: Bennu, Sarus, Amur, Osprey, and Kūaka. They all had a simple objective; find the source of the primion beam. Slow, careful exploration is eschewed for a mad dash over the course of 2 weeks, fueled by friendship, love, and duty to everyone who's slowly dying aboard Nysa's Wake in the meantime.

![break](../../assets/exegesis_thumb.svg)

Sarus is assigned to a path with fractal tunnels; as the largest expeditionary team, they intended to brute force their way through. Several explorers go MIA, seemingly lost to shifts in the tunnels. A few lucky explorers do discover a very promising route which gets them 30km closer to the source of the beam, but like many of the routes, it narrows. luckily, (unlike many of the other routes which appear to have narrowings to single atom width tunnels), at its smallest, it's only 10m wide, which prevents vehicle travel. still, it's the best start yet discovered, so the expedition continues on (thruster assisted) foot from there

![break](../../assets/exegesis_thumb.svg)

Osprey headed down the most direct tunnel that Talus had found, and nearly instantly got fucking killed by an unseen gravitational anomaly, with only the TL surviving. She's absorbed into Amur for the moment. all hands are on

![break](../../assets/exegesis_thumb.svg)

Further beyond, Bennu discovers a void where fusion reactions don't work the hard way; the team was moving at around 100 m/s when they crossed the nondescript membrane of the zone, with only a moment to send an SOS before they all shut down (and crashed, but that only disabled one explorer). SAR Team \{V3N's Team's Name} fetches them by borrowing their fusion core from their shuttle and placing it before the zone, tethering to it as they move in and fetch Bennu. the process is arduous and time consuming since the no-fusion zone is difficult to diagnose. {/* reorganize this section probably in the PoV of the SAR team, where they get an SOS and go to the location and look from afar, seeing nothing particularly wrong. they spend a while just figuring out what the problem is, and then further time setting up the tether */} Bennu was on an straight, easy track, so battery packs are gathered from Delta Site and moved to the start of the void to allow people to make the sprint across.

![break](../../assets/exegesis_thumb.svg)

Amur discovers a zone with incomprehensible, non-euclidian geometry. they get lost for several days, only escaping after writing a few hacks to their navigation. parrots keep getting lost in spaces which are revealed, and then dissapear, further frustrating exploration as their link back to delta is spotty. it's difficult, but eventually realized to be a localized 4 dimensional space, and with cartographic updates, navigating the area is better understood; movement and rotation through certain areas, while appearing to be in 3d, actually has consequential movement and rotation in 4 dimensions, although the way this happens changes on a fixed period. This discovery reveals that the zone seems to be some sort of routing point, with new potential routes revealed

![break](../../assets/exegesis_thumb.svg)

Kūaka, meanwhile, navigates a massive ocean full of a solution of water and a metamaterial comprised of fluidlike nanoparticles, which becomes increasingly opaque to basically all EM radiation as it gets hotter. the nanoparticles appear to basically be like a strange protein that convert heat to EM blocking, for whatever reason. progress is slow, as there are huge masses in the fluid, further confounding navigation, although Kūaka takes advantage of this fluid being enourmously effective as a thermal conductor, allowing for fast movement if they're careful, although an early attempt to use this fluid as propulsion reaction mass results in an explosion comprable to a nuclear weapon. further research reveals that, when overheated and in the presence of amorphous hydrogen and oxygen (such as when water is ionized by a thruster)

Nchọ hadn't had a waking this painful since a weekend morning in university, more than 400 years ago.

![A blinking terminal cursor](/entries/babel_contingency/babel_contingency_0.webp)

The primion event had left them reeling in their seat. Barely able to make sense of left or right, and in too much pain and confusion to realize, they had instinctively fired retrograde thrusters at full power, as if a driver realizing they didn't have control over their car was taught to just hold the brakes and hope for the best. According to telemetry from the black box, the burn lasted for 34 seconds, setting the freighter on a ballistic trajectory which would intersect the Slumbering Empyrean quickly. Some instincts are malformed for spaceflight.

![`/ > boot-up sequence initiated for onye_Nchọgharị`](/entries/babel_contingency/babel_contingency_1.webp)

Obi-me would have pulled emergency control, but the same event had knocked it out; it wouldn't come back online for over 7 minutes after the event, due to its own POST routines not allowing boot up if neural lattice temperature was too high; the titan's synthetic neural patterns had channeled the primion flux into brutal heat, and its automated systems dumped most of its water as superheated steam and then vented it as an emergency control mechanism, a safety which Nchọ's body shared.

The freighter's terrain avoidance system, on the other hand, kicked in at 6 kilometers, flipping the freighter retrograde and burning full tilt. Obi-me had rebooted just 4 seconds before crossing the 6km emergency line, and the titan had little choice or time in the intervening 3 seconds before impact but to grab its human, hold them tight, and brace against a bulkhead.

![`/POST > Power-On Self Test initiated \n `](/entries/babel_contingency/babel_contingency_2.webp)

A splitting headache racked Nchọ. The familiar distress of missing all touch, control, and proprioception of missing a limb, in this case their left leg and entire left arm had been sheared off in the crash, despite Obi-me's best efforts. As they shifted and pushed themselves upright, they felt the horrible crunch and grit of broken shielding, several ceramic panels falling off of their damaged body to the surface of the Slumbering Empyrean below. 

Another old instict, they clutched the crushed remains of their left shoulder, and adjusted their stance by extending their left leg past their usual digitigrade bend, to make up for the lost foot. Looking themselves over, they realized their torso was impaled on a mangled piece of the freighter, passing though the leftmost of their midsection, tearing through the whipple shielding cover and putting a massive hole in one of their water tanks; the water they bled had violently boiled off in the vacuum, and then frozen to nearby surfaces as a bit of frost and snow.

They limped over to the kit, first disconnecting the remnants of their left shoulder and replacing it with the spare left arm, and then unhinging their left ankle to replace it with a new foot and toe, and then filling their water tanks from the reserve.

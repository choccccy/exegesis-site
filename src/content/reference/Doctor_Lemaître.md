# Dr. Agnès Lemaître
Named for Georges Lemaître, a Belgian Catholic priest, theoretical physicist, and mathematician who made major contributions to cosmology and astrophysics - Shortened to **Dr. Lemaître**
*she/her*

## Body
Massive, immobile processing core within a chassis sized to match a standard shipping container for convenience. A cathedral of instruments and cooling mesh, She inhabits multiple lightweight proxy frames simultaneously, precise as marionettes at her command.

## Personality
Poised and analytical, methodical in every gesture. Agnès tempers intellectual rigor with an undertone of melancholy; her humor is dry, her speech laced with *merci*, *hélas*, and other French. Science to her is both salvation and penance—a means to order entropy.

Patient and deliberate, she speaks rarely but with eloquence, her phrases carrying the weight of ancient instruction.

## Speech Pattern
Formal, lightly accented, rhythmically balanced. Interleaves French and English with natural ease; her tone remains measured even under strain.

## Mission Role
Materials scientist and theoretical physicist. Experimental design, data interpretation, and analysis of cosmic anomalies, particularly the Slumbering Empyrean.

## Social Role
Withdrawn, Dr. Lemaître is rarely seen outside of her lab. She provides a (grand)motherly presense to any who come to her space, a patient scientist through and through.
